Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about redevelopment, PMC role, and our advisory services.

General

Redevelopment FAQs

Will society members need to contribute funds?

Our objective is to structure redevelopment with zero or minimal contribution through optimized FSI utilization. We design financial models that aim for full project cost recovery through surplus sale potential, minimizing or eliminating the need for member contributions.

How long does redevelopment take?

Typically 30–36 months, depending on approvals and project scale. The timeline includes pre-construction phases (feasibility, approvals, developer selection) and the actual construction period. Our PMC oversight helps minimize delays.

Do you work for builders?

No. Urban Redevelopment Consultants works exclusively in the interest of societies. We are a society-appointed, independent consultant, ensuring that every recommendation is unbiased and solely focused on protecting member interests.

What is the role of a PMC in redevelopment?

The Project Management Consultant (PMC) acts as the professional representative of the society throughout the redevelopment lifecycle. Our PMC role covers technical due diligence, developer selection, agreement review, execution oversight, and compliance monitoring. Learn more about PMC role →

Why should we appoint a PMC before signing the Development Agreement?

The Development Agreement (DA) is the most irreversible decision in redevelopment. A PMC appointed before DA ensures that all commitments are technically feasible and financially viable. Without PMC involvement, societies risk over-promised deliverables, one-sided clauses, and weak protections. Read more →

What redevelopment models do you handle?

We provide advisory across all prevalent models: Builder-led redevelopment, Society self-redevelopment, Development manager model, Joint redevelopment, and Cluster redevelopment. The best model depends on your society's specific circumstances, land tenure, and goals.

How do you ensure transparency in the process?

We maintain disciplined documentation and governance at every stage. This includes regular member presentations, detailed progress reports, documented decision frameworks, and complete financial transparency. Our independent, conflict-free position ensures unbiased guidance.

What is included in a structural audit?

Our structural audit includes detailed assessment of RCC and load-bearing structures, visual inspection and condition mapping, Non-Destructive Testing (NDT) coordination, residual life assessment, safety grading, and statutory-compliant reporting with repair vs redevelopment recommendations.

Authority-Specific

CIDCO vs NMMC Redevelopment FAQs

Is redevelopment procedure same for CIDCO and NMMC?

No. While redevelopment principles are similar, CIDCO and NMMC follow different land ownership structures, approval authorities, and compliance requirements.

What is the key difference in land ownership?

CIDCO: Land is generally leasehold, governed by CIDCO lease conditions.
NMMC: Land is typically freehold with municipal jurisdiction.
This impacts approval process, documentation, and redevelopment permissions.

Does FSI calculation differ between CIDCO and NMMC?

Yes. Applicable DCPR norms, base FSI, premium FSI, and amenity requirements vary and must be evaluated authority-wise during feasibility.

Is CIDCO No Objection mandatory in CIDCO areas?

Yes. CIDCO approval and compliance with lease conditions are mandatory before proceeding with redevelopment.

Who issues the Occupation Certificate (OC)?

CIDCO areas: OC issued by CIDCO.
NMMC areas: OC issued by NMMC.

Can one redevelopment model work for both CIDCO and NMMC?

Not always. Redevelopment model selection must consider land tenure, approval timelines, premium structures, and authority-specific risks.

Why is authority-specific PMC guidance important?

Because incorrect assumptions at authority level can lead to approval delays, cost overruns, or reduction in member benefits. Urban Redevelopment Consultants ensures redevelopment strategies are authority-aligned from day one. View our authority expertise →

Strategic Importance

The Critical Role of PMC in Redevelopment

In society redevelopment, the Project Management Consultant (PMC) plays a critical fiduciary role, acting as the professional representative of the society throughout the redevelopment lifecycle.

At Urban Redevelopment Consultants, our PMC role is designed to protect member interests, ensure technical and statutory compliance, and maintain financial and execution discipline.

Unlike developer-appointed PMCs, we function as a society-appointed, independent PMC, ensuring that every decision is evaluated through the lens of risk, compliance, and long-term asset value.

Phase-Wise Responsibilities

PMC Role Across Redevelopment Stages

Our involvement spans every critical phase, ensuring governance and accountability throughout.

Phase 1

Pre-Redevelopment (Decision & Planning Stage)

This stage is the most critical, as irreversible decisions are made here. Our focus is on eliminating technical and financial ambiguity before commitments are executed.

  • Structural Audit and Technical Due Diligence
  • Repair vs Redevelopment advisory
  • FSI / TDR / regulatory potential assessment
  • Feasibility studies and financial modeling
  • Society presentations and informed consent support
  • Selection of appropriate redevelopment model
Phase 2

Developer Selection & Agreement Stage

At this stage, the PMC ensures that what is promised is realistically deliverable.

  • Preparation of RFP and tender documents
  • Evaluation of developer credentials and past track record
  • Technical and commercial offer comparison
  • Negotiation support aligned with feasibility assumptions
  • Development Agreement review from PMC perspective
  • Risk identification and mitigation coordination with legal advisors
Phase 3

Design, Approvals & Execution Stage

Continuous oversight to ensure the project stays on track with quality, cost, and compliance objectives.

  • Design coordination between architect, structural engineer, and consultants
  • Monitoring of statutory approvals and conditions
  • Construction quality audits and inspections
  • Timeline and milestone monitoring
  • Cost control and variation review
  • Periodic reporting to managing committee and members
Phase 4

Completion, Handover & Compliance Closure

Ensuring every deliverable meets standards and all compliance requirements are fulfilled.

  • Final inspection and snag list preparation
  • Compliance verification with approved plans
  • Assistance in Occupation Certificate (OC) process
  • Handover documentation review
  • Defect liability period monitoring

Critical Advisory

Why PMC is Required Before Signing the DA

Signing the Development Agreement (DA) is the single most irreversible decision in a redevelopment project.

Once the DA is executed, the society's commercial leverage, technical control, and risk mitigation options are significantly reduced. Appoint a PMC before finalizing the DA.

Key Risks of Signing DA Without PMC Involvement

Over-promised carpet area not supported by FSI or planning norms

Inadequate corpus, rent, or timelines not aligned with feasibility

One-sided clauses favoring the developer

Weak exit, penalty, and delay protection for society

Ambiguity in specifications, amenities, and quality standards

Future disputes due to unclear scope and responsibilities

PMC Value Addition Before DA

Technical Due Diligence

  • Verification of structural audit conclusions
  • Validation of proposed building configuration and member areas
  • Cross-check of FSI, TDR, and regulatory assumptions

Financial & Commercial Validation

  • Alignment of carpet area, corpus, rent, and amenities with feasibility
  • Confirmation that project cost recovery is realistically achievable
  • Sensitivity checks for approval delays and market fluctuations

Risk & Governance Review

  • Identification of technical and execution risks
  • Review of DA clauses from PMC perspective
  • Coordination with legal advisors to plug execution gaps

In redevelopment, a PMC appointed after DA manages construction.
